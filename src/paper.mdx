## Abstract

Model-Based Reinforcement Learning gains sample efficiency through latent imagination, but standard agents remain constrained by **Historical Tethering**: imagination is initialized from observed replay states. This creates a learning asymmetry where the world model discovers the latent manifold faster than the policy can exploit sparse rewards.

**Mind Dreamer (MD)** introduces **Active Causal Intervention**. Instead of always starting imagination from historical data, MD samples generated initial states $s_0 \sim p_{gen}(\cdot)$ to create non-continuous **latent jumps** toward plausible but epistemically challenging regions. Relay Value and Relay Uncertainty functions then propagate pragmatic and epistemic value across these spatial ruptures. Empirically, MD achieves a **1.67x average speedup** over DreamerV3 on DeepMind Control Suite, reaching **8.8x** in sparse-reward tasks.

## The Problem: Historical Tethering

Latent imagination gives model-based agents a powerful internal simulator, but the simulator is usually tethered to the replay buffer. Rollouts begin from states the policy has already visited, so even a capable world model must still wait for slow trajectory-bound exploration to discover reward bottlenecks.

The bottleneck is not that the world model lacks structure, but that standard imagination remains tied to where the agent has already been:

<div className="mx-auto max-w-[42rem]">

| Mechanism | Standard MBRL behavior | Consequence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| World model | Learns local transition geometry from dense self-supervision | The latent manifold can become useful before rewards are discovered |
| Policy learning | Optimizes sparse rewards through trajectory-bound data | Value propagation remains slow around bottlenecks |
| Imagination starts | Rollouts are initialized from replay states $s_0 \sim \mathcal{D}$ | Discovery stays historically tethered to visited regions |

## Mind Dreamer

MD turns the world model from a passive replay machine into an active curriculum generator. The generator proposes synthesized latent anchors; Relay Guidance evaluates whether those anchors are useful, informative, and still physically plausible under the learned manifold.

### Relay Expected Free Energy

The generator is trained to balance exploration, exploitation, and safety:

$$
\max_\theta \mathbb{E}\Big[\eta V_{RVF}(s,s') + \beta V_{RUF}(s,s') - \lambda\mathcal{L}_{mf}(s')\Big].
$$

The **Relay Value Function** treats a generated anchor as an interventional intermediary state:

$$
V_{RVF}(s,s') = \max_{\pi}\mathbb{E}\left[\sum_{t=0}^{k-1}\gamma^t r_t + \gamma^k V_\phi(s')\right].
$$

The **Relay Uncertainty Function** propagates information gain with a quadratic discount:

$$
V_{RUF}(s,s') = \mathbb{E}\left[\sum_{t=0}^{k-1}\gamma^{2t}\mathcal{I}_{t+1} + \gamma^{2k}U_{\phi_u}(s')\right].
$$

The $\gamma^2$ term is not just a tuned coefficient. It follows from variance propagation of informational shocks and gives the generator a finite epistemic horizon, discouraging distal hallucinations.

## Contributions

1. **Active Causal Intervention.** We identify Historical Tethering as a core limitation of model-based RL and introduce generated latent anchors for non-continuous intervention.

2. **Relay Potential Fields.** We derive RVF and RUF to assign value across spatial ruptures, including the $\gamma^2$ epistemic horizon for stable uncertainty propagation.

3. **Theoretical speedup.** We show that MD acts as a variance-minimizing importance sampler that expands the manifold's spectral gap and reduces hitting time to bottleneck states.

4. **Empirical validation.** On DeepMind Control Suite, MD improves both sample efficiency and asymptotic performance over DreamerV3.
